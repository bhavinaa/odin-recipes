Delicious Paneer Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Paneer: 250 gm (store-bought)
  • Onion: 2
  • Tomatoes: 4 (select deep red ones)
  • Ginger: 1 inch
  • Garlic: 6-7 cloves
  • Green chilli: 2
  • Kashmiri Chilly Powder: 1 1/2 to 2 tsp (used 2 tsp)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/4 tsp
  • Garam Masala Powder: 1 tsp
  • Kasuri Methi: 1 tsp
  • Coriander leaves: 1 tbsp chopped (to garnish)
  • Butter: 3 tbsp
  • Bay Leaf: 1
  • Oil: 1 tbsp
  • Cream: 1/4 cup
  • Cashew-nuts: 2 tbsp
  • Sugar: 1 tsp
  • Salt: 1 1/2 tsp or as needed
  • Water: 1 - 1 1/2 cups

To Season Paneer:

  • Butter: 1 tsp
  • Kasuri Methi: One fat pinch
  • Kashmiri Chilly Powder: 1/4 tsp
  • Turmeric powder: A fat pinch

Method:

First soak cashew-nuts and grind to a paste with little water. Keep aside.

Grind the tomatoes in a blender to make about 1 1/2 cups tomato puree.

Chop or mince onion, ginger, and garlic in a food processor.

Heat butter (I used homemade butter) along with 1 tbsp oil. The addition of oil prevents the butter from browning. Add one bay leaf (tej patta).

Add the minced or chopped onion mix. Sauté for a minute or two on medium flame, then add salt.

Reduce the heat to low, allow the onions to brown. Add 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1 1/2 - 2 tsp Kashmiri chilly powder, and 1 tsp garam masala powder. Mix well for a minute to reduce the raw flavor.

Add tomato puree and mix. Increase the heat to high until the puree starts to bubble, then simmer and stir occasionally until oil starts separating.

Meanwhile, heat another pan, preferably nonstick, with 1 tsp butter. Simmer and add 1/4 tsp Kashmiri chilly powder and a pinch of turmeric. Add cubed paneer and toss with a pinch of kasuri methi. Toss for a minute and turn off the heat. This step is optional but recommended to coat the paneer with spices and keep it soft.

Once the tomato gravy has reduced and oil separates, add 1 tsp kasuri methi. Add cashew-nut paste and 1 1/2 cups water to the blender to wash out remaining paste into the gravy.

Bring the gravy to a boil, simmer, add 1 tsp sugar, and add the seasoned paneer. Simmer for 2 minutes, add 1/4 cup cream, stir, and simmer for a minute. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with Tandoori Roti or Hot Phulka.

Notes:

The gravy might thicken; you can add 1/4 cup boiled water to thin it out. Avoid sautéing the paneer too much to keep it soft and moist.
